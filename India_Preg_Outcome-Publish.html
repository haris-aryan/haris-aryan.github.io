Predicting Pregnancy Outcome in Rural Areas Using Machine Learning Methods¶

This analysis is performed using Data published by Natioanal Health Mission - Annual Health Survey¶

"Health is not everything but everything else is nothing without health". Heath infrastructure that has been setup in urban India is far ahead than the lean healthcare facilities available in Rural areas. We have more than 70% of population in rural areas with very basic primary medical facilities and hence they have to travel to cities to avail better medical facilities from private setups which goes heavy on their pockets.

While watching a news report on Social, Economic and Medical challenges faced by HIV patient in rural areas, I picked up this topic to understand :

  1. Pregnancy outcomes in rural India and how it gets impacted from several factors like - ANC and other medical checkups, financial assistance, education of parents etc.
  2. Later I tried to Predict the outcome of pregnancy using same data which can be easily collected at Primary Health care centers

This is a very limited/simple study which gives us confidence that we can build more comprehensive models which can predict the pregnancy outcome in these financially challenged areas based on some generic data. These models might helo in augmenting the tools/capabilities of medical professionals at rural healthcare centers and help them in taking necessary steps in high risk cases.

I am using the NRHM - AHS Survey results for "Bihar" published at https://nrhm-mis.nic.in/hmisreports/AHSReports.aspx

Conclusion

As we have seen above in the data analysis that we have more than 60% pregnancies with even ANC not being done in rural areas (of Bihar). We also see a good correlation between the symptoms and pregnancy outcomes by using very simple machine learning models. Primary healthcare centers are not well equipped with all sophisticated medical facilities, hence if we can make available these machine learning methods (via smart phones or other instruments in offline mode), where healthcare advisors can just feed in the values and get the score for Risk, they would be able to better guide people in rural areas. </I>
